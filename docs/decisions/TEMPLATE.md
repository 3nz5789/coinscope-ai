## Context

What's going on that forces this decision. What exists today. What's changing or missing. Keep it concrete — cite files, metrics, or prior incidents rather than abstractions.

Two or three paragraphs is usually enough. If this section is longer than one page, the decision is probably two decisions in disguise.

## Decision

The choice we are making, stated in one or two sentences. No hedging. A reader skimming this section should know what the ADR commits to.

Follow-on detail (sub-bullets, constraints, rules) goes below the one-line statement.

## Alternatives considered

List the options that lost, each with a one-sentence reason they didn't win.

- **Alternative A** — why it lost.
- **Alternative B** — why it lost.
- **Status quo / do nothing** — why it lost (always include this one).

If there were no real alternatives, the ADR might not be worth writing.
