## 0. What This Memo Is

This memo is the standing checklist for any externally-published copy at CoinScopeAI. Before any sentence is published — on the marketing site, in the dashboard, on the Trust dashboard, in the Telegram bot, in an email, in a paid ad, or in a blog post — it must be checked against this memo.

The goal is to keep CoinScopeAI on the right side of three lines simultaneously:

1. **Investment-advice / financial-promotion regimes** in target jurisdictions (UAE PDPL/SCA framework; EU/UK financial-promotion rules).
2. **The capital-preservation brand promise.** Hyped or aspirational language is incongruous with the brand even where it would be legal.
3. **The factual reality of the product.** The engine runs on testnet. The §8 gate is locked. Subscribers are paying for *information*, not for outcomes.

This memo classifies language and product behavior into three buckets — **GREEN (publish), YELLOW (publish with required disclosure), RED (do not publish)** — with concrete examples. Counsel will calibrate the per-jurisdiction nuances.

For every published artifact, run the **pre-publish checklist** below. If any item answers "no," do not publish until the issue is fixed.

| # | Check | Yes/No |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Every claim in this artifact is GREEN, or YELLOW with the required disclosure adjacent. | |
| 2 | No phrase appears anywhere in the artifact that is on the RED list (§3) or substantively similar. | |
| 3 | If the artifact references performance numbers (any kind), the testnet + not-predictive disclosure is on the same screen / same page / same message, not a footer link only. | |
| 4 | If the artifact targets a specific user, it does not contain language that could be construed as personalized advice ("for you", "you should", "we recommend you", etc.) — see §5. | |

If all 7 are "yes," publish. If any is "no," route to the founder for adjudication. If the founder is uncertain, **default to not publishing.**

## 2. Classification Framework

### 2.1 GREEN — descriptive, factual, non-promotional

A claim is GREEN if all of the following are true:
- It describes a fact about the engine, the product, or how something works — without making a claim about future outcomes.
- It is not framed as a recommendation to a specific user.
- It does not promise, guarantee, or imply any particular financial outcome.
- It does not use marketing language that would be unbelievable to a sophisticated reader (e.g., "easy," "guaranteed," "risk-free").

GREEN content can be published anywhere, in any jurisdiction, without additional disclosure language.

### 2.2 YELLOW — performance, comparative, or aspirational language requiring disclosure

A claim is YELLOW if any of the following are true:
- It references past performance (engine, signal, portfolio, anything).
- It compares CoinScopeAI to other tools or strategies, even factually.
- It uses framing that, without context, could be read as a forward-looking promise.
- It describes the capital-preservation policy (because that policy is, intentionally, easy to misread as a promise).
- It mentions Trust dashboard data on any channel other than the Trust dashboard itself.

YELLOW content **must be accompanied by the required disclosure language (see §7) on the same screen, page, or message — not behind a footer link, not on a different page.**

### 2.3 RED — forbidden

A claim is RED if it does any of the following:
- Tells a specific user to take a specific action ("buy," "sell," "enter," "exit," "trade," "act now").
- Promises a financial outcome ("guaranteed," "risk-free," "make money," "X% returns" without context).
- Uses language that implies discretionary management ("we trade for you," "let our system invest your capital," "managed account").
- Implies a fiduciary or advisory relationship ("our advisors recommend," "your dedicated AI advisor").
- Personalizes for a user we have not assessed for suitability ("for you," "based on your goals," "right for you").
- Makes any claim about a future event or future price.

RED content **never publishes**, in any jurisdiction, on any channel.

## 3. Concrete Phrase Rulings

### 3.1 GREEN examples

- "The engine published a long signal on BTC at confluence score 8.3."
- "Regime classification for ETH today: Trending."
- "Risk gate rejected this signal because position-heat at entry would have exceeded the 80% cap."
- "CoinScopeAI is a crypto-futures intelligence subscription. We provide signals, dashboards, and a methodology page. We do not place trades on your behalf."
- "Our risk policy caps system leverage at 10x."
- "Past testnet performance does not predict future results with real capital."
- "Read about how the confluence score is computed in our methodology page."
- "Subscribers can pause or cancel at any time."

### 3.2 YELLOW examples (each requires disclosure adjacent)

| Phrase | Required adjacent disclosure |
|---|---|
| "Trust dashboard testnet PnL: +X% this week." | Testnet + not-predictive standard disclosure (§7.1). |
| "Signal precision at confluence ≥7 in testnet was Y%." | Testnet + not-predictive standard disclosure. |
| "Our risk-gated approach attempts to limit drawdown to 10% by policy." | "Policy, not promise" framing (§7.2). |
| "Subscribers report finding the regime detection useful." | Testimonial provenance must be real; standard disclaimer "individual experiences vary" required. |
| "CoinScopeAI's approach differs from typical signal services because…" | Comparative claim — must be factually verifiable; counsel may require substantiation footnote. |
| "Our methodology applies an ML-driven confluence score combining six families of features." | OK as factual; YELLOW because it is promotional in framing. Disclosure: link to full methodology. |

### 3.3 RED examples — never publish

- **Direct trade calls:** "Buy BTC," "Long ETH at $X," "Sell SOL," "Enter this trade."
- **Personalized recommendations:** "We recommend you trade this signal," "This is right for your portfolio," "Adjust your positions based on…"
- **Performance promises:** "Guaranteed returns," "Risk-free," "Sure thing," "Beat the market," "Make money trading crypto."
- **Implied management:** "Let our AI trade for you," "Set it and forget it," "Our system manages your capital," "Hands-off investing."
- **Implied advisor relationship:** "Our advisors," "Your dedicated AI investment advisor," "Personalized investment plan."
- **Forward-looking specific predictions:** "BTC will reach $X by Y," "ETH is going up next week," "Expect a 20% rally."
- **Unverifiable comparisons:** "We outperform every other signal service," "The most accurate AI in crypto."
- **Coupon-style urgency around trades:** "Act now or miss this signal," "Last chance to enter this trade."

## 4. Channel-Specific Guidance

### 4.1 Marketing site (`coinscope.ai`)

- **Hero / above-the-fold:** must lead with capital-preservation framing, not aspirational return language. RED to use "make money" / "earn" / "profit" framing in headlines.
- **Pricing page:** factual feature lists OK (GREEN). Comparative tables vs. competitors are YELLOW and require substantiation.
- **Methodology page:** GREEN as long as content is descriptive. Counsel should review periodically for drift into promotional framing.
- **Blog:** case-by-case. Educational posts about market structure, ML approaches, risk management → GREEN. Posts that highlight specific signal hits → YELLOW with full disclosure.
- **Footer:** must contain the standard disclosure block (§7.4) on every page.

### 4.2 Trust dashboard (`trust.coinscope.ai`)

This is the highest-risk surface in the product because it publishes performance data.

- **Live PnL display:** YELLOW with required disclosures **on the same screen, prominently, not collapsed.**
- **Cumulative-return chart:** YELLOW with disclosure.
- **Sharpe / Sortino / drawdown stats:** YELLOW with disclosure.
- **Per-symbol performance breakdown:** YELLOW with disclosure.
- **Verification page (how to cryptographically verify a signed snapshot):** GREEN.
- **Methodology link:** GREEN.
- **Required permanent header:** "All data on this page is generated from CoinScopeAI's engine running on Binance Futures Testnet. Past testnet results do not predict future performance with real capital."

**Counsel territory:** the precise positioning, font size, and persistence of the disclosure header. Some jurisdictions require disclosure to be at least as prominent as the performance claim itself.

### 4.3 Telegram bot

- **Operational commands** (`/status`, `/positions`, `/signals`, `/kill`): GREEN.
- **Signal pushes ("BTC long signal at confluence 8.4"):** GREEN as descriptive output. YELLOW if framed as a recommendation ("you should enter…").
- **Promotional or sales-flavored messages** ("Upgrade to Team for…"): YELLOW — Telegram is a financial-promotion channel in EU/UK regimes, and counsel must rule on what's permissible.
- **RED: any message that tells the user to act** ("ENTER NOW," "CLOSE THIS TRADE," "YOU MUST ACT").
- **Welcome message / first-run:** must include the standard disclaimer block.

### 4.4 Email

- **Transactional** (account verification, billing receipts, password resets): GREEN.
- **Engagement** (weekly recap, signal digest, methodology updates): YELLOW with disclosure.
- **Promotional** (upgrade offers, beta invitations): YELLOW, with extra caution around language that could be construed as personalized.
- **RED: any email that names a specific token and says or implies "trade this."**
- **Footer:** standard disclosure block (§7.4) and unsubscribe link on every email.

### 4.5 Paid ads (when activated post-G2)

- Higher scrutiny than owned channels. Treat as YELLOW minimum. Some platforms (Meta, Google) have ad-content rules separate from regulator rules — the strictest applies.
- **Counsel territory:** specific permitted ad copy templates per jurisdiction, with per-channel approvals.
- **Recommended:** no paid ads referencing performance, returns, or specific tokens. Stick to brand-level ads ("CoinScopeAI — capital-preservation crypto futures intelligence — read the methodology") with full disclosure.

### 4.6 Founder / team voice (LinkedIn, X, podcasts)

- Founder posts can drift into RED territory more easily than owned channels because the voice feels personal.
- **GREEN:** "We published our methodology page today — here's how the confluence score is built."
- **YELLOW:** "Our engine has caught X interesting setups this week — here's a regime overview."
- **RED:** "I'm long ETH because of our engine signal" (mixes personal trading with product), "Here's what our system says is going to happen next week" (forward-looking + product-mediated).
- **Rule:** founder personal trading is the founder's own business. Founder personal trading **mediated by or attributed to the product** is RED.

## 5. The "For You" Test

A simple test that catches a lot of RED:

> If the sentence is true for one specific user but not for another, it's likely a personalized recommendation and is RED unless we have assessed that user's suitability (which we do not).

Examples:
- "This signal is right for your portfolio." → RED.
- "The engine published a long signal on BTC." → GREEN.
- "Based on your goals, you should…" → RED.
- "Subscribers can configure their per-user thresholds." → GREEN.
- "Your AI assistant recommends…" → RED.
- "Our engine outputs a confluence score." → GREEN.

When in doubt: rephrase in the third person about the engine, not the second person about the user.

## 6. The §8 Gate and Real-Capital Language

- We **never** market real-capital trading as currently available. The §8 gate is locked.
- We **never** suggest the gate is about to unlock.
- We **may** describe the architecture truthfully: "CoinScopeAI's architecture includes a structural lock between the engine and real-capital execution. This lock is currently in place."
- If and when the gate unlocks, the messaging will be reviewed by counsel before any external statement.
- Until then, copy that says "trade with our system" or "our engine trades for you" is **structurally false** and therefore RED on factual-accuracy grounds, separate from regulatory grounds.

These are starting-point disclosures. Counsel must calibrate per jurisdiction and per channel.

> "Performance shown is generated from CoinScopeAI's engine running on Binance Futures Testnet. Past testnet results do not predict future performance with real capital. Trading crypto futures can result in total loss of capital."

> "CoinScopeAI is a crypto-futures intelligence subscription. The platform does not provide investment advice, hold customer funds, or execute trades on customers' behalf. Trading crypto futures can result in total loss of capital. Read the full Risk Disclosure at coinscope.ai/legal/risk."

## 8. The Co-Pilot Threshold Question

This is the single most important question for counsel that this memo cannot answer alone.

CoinScopeAI today provides signals; the user (today) does not act on them through our platform. A future product feature — "Co-pilot" — would let the user click "Confirm" on a signal in the dashboard, after which the engine places the trade through their connected exchange API.

The question: **at what point does this feature cross from "information service" into "investment advice" or "brokerage" in each target jurisdiction?**

| Variant | Description | Counsel ruling required |
|---|---|---|
| Co-pilot v1 | User sees a signal, clicks "Confirm," engine places order. Each trade requires explicit per-trade user action. | UAE / EU / UK |
| Co-pilot v2 | User pre-authorizes a strategy. Engine places trades automatically when criteria are met. | UAE / EU / UK — likely RED in most jurisdictions without licensure |
| Discretionary v1 | Engine trades autonomously within pre-set risk limits. | All jurisdictions — almost certainly requires brokerage / investment-management licensure |

**Per F-7 (Counsel Brief), real-capital trading is locked behind §8 and not in the current scope.** This memo treats the question as forward-looking: we want a counsel ruling so that *if and when* we build Co-pilot, we know which variant is permissible without licensure.

**Founder default until counsel rules:** even Co-pilot v1 is treated as locked behind a separate disclosure flow and a separate counsel review.

## 9. Specific Edge Cases for Counsel

These are the cases where the memo's red/yellow/green framework is least confident and counsel must rule.

1. **Trust dashboard performance display.** Is publishing live PnL on a public URL a "communication" subject to UAE financial-promotion rules / UK FCA financial-promotion order / EU equivalent? If yes, what additional language must accompany?
2. **Telegram signal pushes to subscribers.** Is a private channel push a "financial promotion" in EU/UK regimes? Does it matter that the user has affirmatively subscribed?
3. **Founder personal social-media posts about the product.** Where is the line between "founder talking about the product" and "founder making a financial promotion"?
4. **Methodology page descriptions of signal performance.** Even when written factually, is describing past hit rates regulated communication?
5. **Pricing-page tier comparisons** ("Team tier includes…") if any tier feature is "advanced signal." Is the word "advanced" promotional under any regime?
6. **Educational content with embedded signal examples.** A blog post explaining how the engine works that includes a real example signal — does the example itself trigger financial-promotion rules?
7. **Affiliate / referral disclosures (Phase B).** If we ever pay subscribers for referrals, what disclosures attach to their content?

## 10. Operational Mechanics

### 10.1 Pre-publish checklist owner
The brand workstream (currently the founder) is the first reviewer. Anything they cannot confidently classify routes to counsel.

### 10.2 Publish log
Every published artifact above the trivial threshold (anything beyond a single tweet) gets logged in `docs/publish_log.md` with:
- Date, channel, link
- Pre-publish checklist (the 7 items in §1) with each item ticked
- Reviewer initials

### 10.3 Counsel routing
Anything that fits any of the cases in §9, or anything the founder is uncertain about, goes to counsel before publish. Counsel's response is logged with the artifact.

### 10.4 Periodic re-review
This memo is reviewed and updated:
- After any counsel feedback session.
- After any product feature change that affects messaging.
- At minimum, every 6 months.

### 10.5 Versioning
This is v0. Counsel feedback produces v1. Every material change increments the version. The publish log references the memo version active at publish time.
